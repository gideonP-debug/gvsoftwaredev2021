Cloud Computing:

  • Cloud computing involves using networks of remote servers to store and manage data, as opposed to using a personal computer or local server for storage.
  • Rather than owning their own computing infrastructure or data centers, companies rent access to anything from a cloud service provider. This allows companies to avoid upfront costs of owning and maintaining their own data infrastructure.
  • Some examples of cloud computing in our everyday lives: the cloud back-up of photos on your phone, cloud storage for emails, video streaming for applications such as Hulu or Netflix.
  • The cloud computing method of delivering a service over the internet is becoming preferred as opposed to offering standalone products.

Scrum:

  • Scrum is an agile framework, used most frequently by software development teams, that helps groups work together, and structure and manage their work load.
  • The scrum framework is based on continuous learning - the work team does not know everything at the beginning of the project, but their knowledge will evolve with time and experience with the project. To be consistent and successful, it is important to have strong communication, transparency, and a dedication to continuous improvement. Scrum uses visual methods to track work progress, placing an emphasis on efficiency and splitting complex tasks into smaller, more manageable, pieces of work (called sprints).
  • Scrum Artifacts:
    • Product Backlog - maintained by the product owner, it is a dynamic master "To-Do" list for the team
    • Sprint Backlog - the list of items selected by the development team during the implementation of the current sprint cycle
    • Increment - the usable end product from a sprint session, what is reached when the project or sprint is completed
  • Essential Scrum Roles:
    • Product Owner - in charge of understanding the business, the customers, and the market requirements in orer to prioritize work order for the team
    • Master - act as coaches for the product owner, team members, and the business to fine-tune the scrum processes of the group
    • Development Team - drives the plan for each sprint, working as a team to maintain organization and ensure a successful sprint completion.
