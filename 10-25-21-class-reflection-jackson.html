Cloud Computing:

  • The technology of cloud computing has seen an increase in popularity among individuals and business over the past few years. Cloud computing has three service models layered based on computing infrastructure. The three service models of the cloud computing infrastructure are: Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (Paas), and Infrastructure as a Service (Iaas). Each of the three models allows users to use cloud computing in a way that is specific to the individual user. Individuals who use the SaaS model are able to access the application through a client interface such as a web browser. The providers of the SaaS model would be the ones who are responsible for maintaining the cloud from its physical servers to the operating systems. The common products of the SaaS system are Google Apps and Salesforce Sales CRM. On the other hand, the PaaS service model permits businesses and individuals to use their own applications, such as programming language and tools, on the providerâ€™s cloud infrastructure. Within this service model, the service providers are responsible for managing everything except the application. The common products provided through the PaaS model are Google AppEngine, Windows Azure, and Joyent. Finally, under the IaaS service model, users are able to deploy and run software as operating systems and applications through the management of storage, network, processing and fundamental computing resources. Typical providers of the IaaS system include Amazon, Linode, Rackspace, Joyent, and IBM Blue Cloud (Durkee).

Agile:

  • Agile is an incremental and iterative approach; Waterfall is a linear and sequential approach.
  • Agile separates a project into sprints; Waterfall divides a project into phases.
  • Agile helps complete many small projects; Waterfall helps complete one single project
  • Agile introduces a product mindset with a focus on customer satisfaction; Waterfall focuses on successful project delivery.
  • Requirements are prepared everyday in Agile, while requirements are prepared once at the start in Waterfall.
  • Agile allows requirement changes at any time; Waterfall avoids scope changes once the project starts
  • Testing is performed concurrently with development in Agile; testing phase comes only after the build phase in a Waterfall project.
  • Test teams in Agile can take part in requirements change; test teams in Waterfall do not get involved in requirements change
  • Agile enables the project team to operate without a dedicated project manager; Waterfall requires a project manager who plays an essential role in every phase.
