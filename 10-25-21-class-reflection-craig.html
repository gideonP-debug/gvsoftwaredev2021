Back-End Development:

Back-end development is what is going on behind the scenes of an application. Back-end developers build, maintain, and debug the back end that runs an application. The back-end of a website consists of a server, an application, and a database. If you want to be a back-end developer, a good understanding of databases, server programming languages and architecture is a must.

Scrum:

Scrum is a structured agile approach. With scrum, a team promises to deliver some increment of work by the end of each set time interval. These short time intervals (usually two to four weeks) are known as sprints. The short time frame of a sprint forces complex tasks to be split into smaller stories and help your team learn and accomplish these tasks quickly. These sprints also contain regular meetings with your team to update each other on the progress that has been made.

Scrum teams have three defined roles:

  • The Product Owner - advocates for the customer, manages the product backlog, and helps prioritize the work done by the development team.
  • The Scrum Master - helps the team stay grounded in the scrum principles.
  • The Development Team - chooses the work to be done, delivers increments, and demonstrates collective accountability.
